Nike's Timeline

Blue Ribbon Sports Founded

1964

Phil Knight and Bill Bowerman start Blue Ribbon Sports (BRS) to distribute Japanese Onitsuka Tiger running shoes in the U.S.

Blue Ribbon Sports Founded

Phil Knight and his coach Bill Bowerman establish Blue Ribbon Sports on January 25, 1964, to import and sell Onitsuka Tiger running shoes. Operating initially out of Knight’s car at track meets, they moved from $8,000 in first-year sales to around $20,000 by 1965—setting the stage for future growth.

The Nike Name & Swoosh

1971

BRS officially becomes Nike, Inc. The iconic Swoosh logo is created by graphic design student Carolyn Davidson for just $35.

The Nike Name & Swoosh

After parting ways with Onitsuka Tiger, the company rebrands to Nike, Inc. on May 30, 1971, named after the Greek goddess of victory. That same year, Carolyn Davidson, a graphic design student, creates the iconic “Swoosh” logo for just $35; Phil Knight later rewards her with shares.

First Nike Shoes Launched

1972

Nike introduces its first line of sneakers, including the "Moon Shoe," famously made with a waffle iron sole.

First Nike Shoes Launched

Bill Bowerman designs the original running shoe known as the Nike Cortez, aimed at U.S. Olympic athletes. Debuting at the peak of the 1972 Summer Olympics, it became a massive success—selling approximately $800,000 in its first year—and helped establish Nike’s reputation for performance footwear.

Michael Jordan Partnership

1984

Nike signs rookie Michael Jordan, launching the Air Jordan line — a revolutionary and cultural phenomenon.

Michael Jordan Partnership

In October 1984, Nike secures a groundbreaking five-year, $2.5 million deal with rookie Michael Jordan. The Air Jordan I launches in April 1985. Despite being “banned” for not adhering to NBA uniform rules, Nike capitalizes on the controversy, paying the fines and turning the shoes into a cultural phenomenon, generating $150 million in sales in its first year.

"Just Do It" Campaign

1988

The iconic slogan "Just Do It" debuts, becoming one of the most recognized brand mottos in history.

"Just Do It" Campaign

Nike collaborates with ad agency Wieden+Kennedy to launch the “Just Do It” slogan in July 1988. This motivational catchphrase resonated widely, defining Nike's ethos and becoming a cornerstone in global advertising history.

Tiger Woods Joins Nike

1996

Nike expands further into sports marketing by signing golf prodigy Tiger Woods, transforming Nike Golf.

Tiger Woods Joins Nike

After dominating the amateur golf scene and winning the 1996 Masters, Tiger Woods signs with Nike, catapulting the brand to prominence in golf equipment and apparel. His presence transformed Nike Golf into a key player in the sport.

Phil Knight Steps Down as CEO

2004

Co-founder Phil Knight steps aside, and Nike continues global expansion under new leadership.

Phil Knight Steps Down as CEO

Co-founder Phil Knight steps away from CEO duties on December 28, 2004, as Nike transitions to a new generation of leadership. Under his stewardship, the company evolved from a small running shoe distributor into the world’s largest athletic footwear and apparel brand.

Flyknit Innovation

2012

Nike launches Flyknit technology—lightweight, eco-friendly knitted sneakers changing shoe design.

Flyknit Innovation

LNike debuts the Flyknit technology with the Flyknit Racer and Trainer at the 2012 London Olympics. This revolutionary knit-upper approach delivered race-ready, ultra-lightweight sneakers with minimal waste—ushering in a new era of sustainable design. By the end of that year, Flyknit materials were applied across diverse categories including hoops, soccer, and lifestyle sneakers.

Move to Digital & Sustainability

2020

Nike accelerates digital transformation and commits to sustainability through its Move to Zero campaign.

Move to Digital & Sustainability

Around 2020, Nike intensifies its Move to Zero campaign, committing to carbon neutrality and zero waste. Simultaneously, the brand accelerates its digital services with the SNKRS app, Nike+ ecosystem, and personalized platforms—responding to shifting consumer behaviors.

Nike Air & Future Tech

2023

Nike continues pushing innovation with smart shoes, AR experiences, and expanding its Nike Air legacy.

Nike Air & Future Tech

In the 2020s, Nike continues to push advancements in smart footwear, augmented reality experiences, and its Nike Air line legacy. Though specific 2023 events are not yet documented, the brand is actively exploring self-lacing systems, embedded sensors, and AR try-ons—signaling its trajectory into future-forward retail and product experiences.
