Nike's Timeline

Blue Ribbon Sports Founded

1964

Phil Knight and Bill Bowerman start Blue Ribbon Sports (BRS) to distribute Japanese Onitsuka Tiger running shoes in the U.S.

Blue Ribbon Sports Founded

Phil Knight and his coach Bill Bowerman establish Blue Ribbon Sports on January 25, 1964, to import and sell Onitsuka Tiger running shoes. Operating initially out of Knight’s car at track meets, they moved from $8,000 in first-year sales to around $20,000 by 1965—setting the stage for future growth.

The Nike Name & Swoosh

1971

BRS officially becomes Nike, Inc. The iconic Swoosh logo is created by graphic design student Carolyn Davidson for just $35.

The Nike Name & Swoosh

After parting ways with Onitsuka Tiger, the company rebrands to Nike, Inc. on May 30, 1971, named after the Greek goddess of victory. That same year, Carolyn Davidson, a graphic design student, creates the iconic “Swoosh” logo for just $35; Phil Knight later rewards her with shares
